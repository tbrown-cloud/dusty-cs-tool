Renewal · Pre-call prep
Full account snapshot before renewal
Give me a complete usage and health snapshot for [ACCOUNT NAME] ahead of their renewal on [DATE]. Include: total linear feet laid and robot hours over the contract period, month-over-month usage trend, number of projects and robots deployed, any usage dips or gaps, and their productivity rate compared to our average customer. Flag anything that could come up as a risk or objection in the renewal conversation.

Renewal · ROI framing
Build the ROI case without "time savings"
For [ACCOUNT NAME], help me build a business value narrative that goes beyond time savings. Using their usage data, show me: (1) how much layout work their team has completed with Dusty vs what a manual crew would have needed to deliver the same output, (2) which projects had the highest output density suggesting Dusty enabled work that would have been hard to staff manually, (3) whether their project count or scope has grown since they started with Dusty. Frame this as labor reallocation and capacity expansion, not hours saved.

Renewal · Price objection
Prepare for "Dusty is priced too high"
I'm going into a renewal with [ACCOUNT NAME] and I expect a price objection. Pull the strongest data points I can use to justify the investment — their busiest months, their highest-output projects, total linear feet laid over the contract period. Then help me frame the cost not as a software subscription but as the cost of a high-precision layout capability they couldn't staff manually. Give me 3 specific data-backed talking points and one question I can ask them to get them to articulate the value themselves.

Pipeline · Project gap risk
Project pipeline slowing — get ahead of it
Usage for [ACCOUNT NAME] appears to be slowing or their active project count is declining. Before I get on the phone with them, help me understand: is this a natural project gap between jobs, or a sign of disengagement? What does their historical project cadence look like — how long have gaps typically lasted before? What projects do they have in their SFDC pipeline that could be Dusty candidates? Give me language to open a proactive conversation about their upcoming pipeline without sounding like I'm worried about churn.
