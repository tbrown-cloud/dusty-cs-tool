Renewal Conversations
Renewal · Pre-call prep
Full account snapshot before renewal
Give me a complete usage and health snapshot for [ACCOUNT NAME] ahead of their renewal on [DATE]. Include: total linear feet laid and robot hours over the contract period, month-over-month usage trend, number of projects and robots deployed, any usage dips or gaps, and their productivity rate compared to our average customer. Flag anything that could come up as a risk or objection in the renewal conversation.
renewalpre-callhealth
Renewal · ROI framing
Build the ROI case without "time savings"
For [ACCOUNT NAME], help me build a business value narrative that goes beyond time savings. Using their usage data, show me: (1) how much layout work their team has completed with Dusty vs what a manual crew would have needed to deliver the same output, (2) which projects had the highest output density suggesting Dusty enabled work that would have been hard to staff manually, (3) whether their project count or scope has grown since they started with Dusty. Frame this as labor reallocation and capacity expansion, not hours saved.
renewalROIvalue
Renewal · Objection prep
Prepare for "we're not sure we're getting value"
I'm going into a renewal with [ACCOUNT NAME] and I expect a "not sure we're getting value" objection. Pull the strongest data points I can use to counter this — their busiest months, their highest-output projects, any periods where usage accelerated. Also flag: are there projects where they clearly relied heavily on Dusty? What would have been difficult to deliver without it? Give me 3-4 specific data-backed talking points I can use in the meeting.
renewalobjection handling
QBR / Business Reviews
QBR · Opening story
Tell the story of this quarter in 3 highlights
Summarize [ACCOUNT NAME]'s Dusty usage for [Q + YEAR] in 3 headline moments I can open a QBR with. Each highlight should be a specific, concrete data point — their biggest output month, their most-used robot or project, a productivity milestone. Make each one feel like something worth celebrating, not just a number. Format as three short punchy statements I can put on a slide.
QBRstorytellingslides
QBR · Trend analysis
Usage trend and what's driving it
For [ACCOUNT NAME], show me the usage trend over the last [3/6/12] months and help me explain what's driving it. Is usage growing, plateauing, or dipping? Which projects are driving the most activity? Are there signs that they're expanding how they use Dusty (more robots, new project types, new sites)? Give me a narrative I can walk through with the customer that shows we understand their business, not just their usage metrics.

At-Risk Accounts
At-risk · Diagnosis
Why is usage down — and what do I do about it?
Usage for [ACCOUNT NAME] has dropped in the last [TIMEFRAME]. Help me diagnose why before I get on the phone with them. Show me: when the drop started, which projects or robots went quiet, whether this looks like a project gap (they finished a job and haven't started the next) or a real disengagement signal. Then suggest 2-3 questions I can ask on the call to figure out what's actually going on without sounding like I'm reading from a dashboard.
at-riskchurndiagnosis
At-risk · Save conversation
Build a re-engagement case
I need to re-engage [ACCOUNT NAME] who has been underusing Dusty. Pull their strongest historical usage data — their best months, their most successful projects, their peak productivity rate. I want to remind them of what good looked like when they were fully engaged, and contrast it with where they are now. Help me frame this as "here's what your team was able to do" rather than "here's why you're not using it enough."

Expansion Conversations
Expansion · Upsell trigger
Find the signal that they're ready for more
For [ACCOUNT NAME], look for signals that they may be hitting capacity constraints with their current Dusty deployment. Are there months where robot utilization was unusually high? Projects where they had to sequence work that could have run in parallel with a second robot? A growing backlog of project types they haven't deployed Dusty on yet? Give me the 2-3 strongest signals I can use to open an expansion conversation naturally, without it feeling like a sales push.
expansionupsellgrowth
Expansion · New project types
Where are they NOT using Dusty yet?
Based on [ACCOUNT NAME]'s current project portfolio in FunnelStory and their Dusty usage history, identify project types or sites where they are active but haven't deployed Dusty. Cross-reference their SFDC opportunity data with their usage data. Where are the gaps? Which of those gaps represent the most natural expansion opportunity based on project size, complexity, or similarity to projects where they've already had success with Dusty?
